# World Building Guide

## Visual motif rules

- `style.zone.machine_silo`: guided industrial salvage, readable machine silhouettes, painted route cues
- `style.zone.sky_docks`: open sightlines, freight-runway framing, bright extraction landmarks
- `style.zone.relay_tunnels`: compressed interiors, relay coils, hidden shortcut language

## Prop language

- machinery should read as repairable, not disposable
- landmarks should be large enough to guide a child through the route quickly
- props should reinforce room purpose: loot, danger, traversal, extraction, or shortcut

## Color hierarchy

- cool guidance colors for safe movement paths
- warm reward colors for loot and maker-success beats
- red or amber danger cues for combat and timed pressure
- extraction beacons must remain readable even in cluttered scenes

## Signage grammar

- arrows point to primary flow
- chevrons mark extraction and return-safe surfaces
- bay letters and relay node labels identify hubs and traversal branches
- signage must not depend on tiny text alone

## Faction and device style rules

- machine-silo devices use service markings and patched repairs
- sky-dock devices use freight and landing-strip motifs
- relay-tunnel devices use maintenance glyphs and conduit labels

## Phase 5 rule

- every room variant and mission blueprint should map cleanly to one style profile and one review record before it is marked shippable
